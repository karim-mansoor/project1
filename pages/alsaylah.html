Al Saylah is an ancient water system that runs through the heart of the historic city of Sana'a, Yemen. This intricate system of underground channels, wells, and cisterns has been collecting and distributing rainwater throughout the city for over 1,000 years. The channels are fed by natural springs and canals that bring water from the surrounding mountains. The system is a testament to the ingenuity and resourcefulness of the people of Sana'a and is an essential part of the city's cultural heritage. As you explore the narrow streets of Old Sana'a, you'll likely come across the traditional wooden doors that lead to the entrances of Al Saylah, a hidden and mysterious world that has sustained life in the city for centuries. The water system is a beautiful example of the symbiotic relationship between humans and nature and is a must-see attraction for anyone visiting Sana'a.
